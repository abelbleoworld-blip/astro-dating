§ 02 — research

SOL-1 — forecast of a grand solar minimum

Five paleoclimate proxies over 55,000 years, 6 solar cycles, 500 Monte Carlo runs. The Gleissberg cycle (88 years) is confirmed 4/4.

Key result

95.4% probability of a deep + grand solar minimum.

Median — around the year 2126. Be-10 × C-14 correlation r = 0.47. Probability of "nothing happens" — exactly 0.0%.

Code is open. Data is public. We do not ask you to believe — we ask you to verify.

Dating the Almagest

Extension of the Dambis–Efremov method (JHA, 2000) from 8 to 1022 stars of Ptolemy's catalogue. RMS residual 1.23°. The Hipparchus epoch (~120 BCE) is confirmed. The medieval-origin hypothesis is excluded.

Hipparcos + Gaia DR3 Verbunt & van Gent 2012 GitHub v1.0
Completed Verification · 5 continents
16 civilizations · 18 anchors

Astronomical verification of chronology: from the Bur-Sagale eclipse (763 BCE, accuracy 0.028°) to the supernova of 1054 CE (5 cultures). 30 apparitions of Halley's Comet. The Maya Dresden Codex (Venus: 583.92 days, error 0.02%). All 18 anchors agree with JPL ephemerides.
