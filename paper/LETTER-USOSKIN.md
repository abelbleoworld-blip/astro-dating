# Letter to Prof. Usoskin — English version

> Полная версия (русская, +15% содержания): [[LETTER-USOSKIN-RU]]

Subject: Multi-proxy solar activity reconstruction (55 kyr) — request for feedback

Dear Prof. Usoskin,

I am writing to share a preprint that builds directly on the body of work you have extensively reviewed in your Living Reviews in Solar Physics article on solar activity over millennia.

We have constructed a five-proxy pipeline (SOL-1) combining:
- Pre-telescopic sunspot records (Yau & Stephenson 1988)
- Auroral catalogs (Silverman 1992, Keimatsu 1970)
- Eclipse corona morphology descriptions (Stephenson 1997)
- Radiocarbon Δ¹⁴C (IntCal20, Reimer et al. 2020)
- ¹⁰Be concentration (GISP2, Finkel & Nishiizumi 1997)

FFT spectral analysis independently confirms the Gleissberg cycle (~88 yr) in 4 of 4 applicable proxies (E1: 88.2, E2: 87.5, E3: 88.8, E4: 87.6 yr). Cross-validation between detrended ¹⁰Be and Δ¹⁴C yields Pearson r = 0.47 over 37 kyr.

A six-component harmonic model phase-fitted to five historically documented Grand Solar Minima produces a Monte Carlo forecast (500 realizations): median next GSM at ~2126 CE (68% CI: 2058–2206), P(Deep+Grand) = 95.4%.

Our result is most consistent with Steinhilber & Beer (2013) at 2090 ± 30 CE, using a comparable cosmogenic isotope methodology but with five proxies instead of two.

We would greatly value your expert assessment of this work — particularly regarding the limitations of harmonic superposition versus nonlinear dynamo models for this forecast horizon, and whether the eclipse corona morphology proxy (E3) has been previously used in systematic reconstructions.
