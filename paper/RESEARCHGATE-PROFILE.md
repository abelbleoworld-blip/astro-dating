## About / Bio

Researcher at GPNTB Russia (Russian National Public Library for Science and Technology), Network Scientific Laboratory (NSL) for Digital Transformation and AI, GPNTB Russia, Moscow. Background in data analysis and computational methods.

Current project: **astro-dating** — a computational archaeoastronomy pipeline combining five independent solar activity proxies spanning 55,000 years to forecast the next Grand Solar Minimum, alongside astronomical verification of historical chronology across 16 civilizations on 5 continents.

Key result: 95.4% probability of a significant solar activity decline before 2200 CE (median ~2126), consistent with Steinhilber & Beer 2013 (ETH Zurich). All code and data are open source (CC BY 4.0).

## Publications to Upload

### 1. Primary Paper (upload as Preprint)

**Title:** AI-driven multi-proxy predictive system for solar activity over 55,000 years and probabilistic forecast of the next Grand Solar Minimum using Monte Carlo simulation

**Authors:** Dmitriev, A.A.

**Abstract:** We present a five-stage analysis pipeline (SOL-1) combining pre-telescopic sunspot records, auroral catalogs, eclipse corona morphology, radiocarbon (14C, IntCal20), and cosmogenic beryllium (10Be, GISP2) to identify and cross-validate solar activity periodicities over 55,000 years. Fourier analysis independently confirms the Gleissberg cycle (~88 yr) in 4 of 4 applicable proxies. Monte Carlo simulation (500 realizations) yields a median next Grand Solar Minimum at ~2126 CE (68% CI: 2058–2206), with 95% probability of at least a "Deep" minimum.
