Subject: Extension of your 2013 solar activity forecast using 5-proxy pipeline

Dear Dr. Steinhilber,

Your 2013 paper with Dr. Beer ("Prediction of solar activity for the next 500 years," JGR Space Physics) has been a key reference for our work. We have extended your cosmogenic isotope approach by adding three additional observational proxies.

Our SOL-1 pipeline combines:
1. IntCal20 Δ¹⁴C (9,501 points, 55 kyr) — as in your work
2. GISP2 ¹⁰Be (387 points, 40 kyr) — as in your work
3. Pre-telescopic sunspot records (112 observations, Yau 1988) — new
4. Auroral catalogs (~2,000 records, Silverman/Keimatsu) — new
5. Eclipse corona morphology (116 eclipses, Stephenson 1997) — new

Cross-validation confirms Gleissberg (~88 yr) in 4/4 proxies. ¹⁰Be–Δ¹⁴C correlation: r = 0.47.

Our Monte Carlo forecast (500 realizations): median GSM at ~2126 CE (68% CI: 2058–2206), which is consistent with your prediction of 2090 ± 30 CE. The additional proxies narrow the uncertainty and provide independent confirmation.

We would welcome your feedback, particularly on:
- Whether adding pre-telescopic direct observations meaningfully constrains the forecast
- How the harmonic superposition compares to your PCA-based approach
- Any concerns about the eclipse corona morphology as a proxy
