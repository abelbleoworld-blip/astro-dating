Dear Prof. Usoskin,

Following your Living Reviews in Solar Physics review on millennial-scale solar activity (2017), I am writing to share a preprint that extends this methodology.

The SOL-1 pipeline integrates five proxies:

- pre-telescopic sunspot records (Yau & Stephenson 1988);
- auroral catalogues (Silverman 1992; Keimatsu 1970);
- eclipse corona morphology (Stephenson 1997);
- radiocarbon Δ¹⁴C (IntCal20; Reimer et al. 2020);
- ¹⁰Be from the GISP2 ice core (Finkel & Nishiizumi 1997).

FFT spectral analysis confirms the Gleissberg cycle (~88 yr) in four of four applicable observational proxies (E1: 88.2; E2: 87.5; E3: 88.8; E4: 87.6 yr). Cross-validation between detrended ¹⁰Be and Δ¹⁴C yields Pearson r = 0.47 over 37 kyr.

A six-component harmonic model phase-fitted to five historically documented Grand Solar Minima produces a Monte Carlo forecast (500 realisations): median next GSM at ~2126 CE (68 % CI: 2058–2206), P(Deep + Grand) = 95.4 %. The result is consistent with Steinhilber & Beer (2013): 2090 ± 30 CE, using five proxies instead of two cosmogenic ones.

I would be grateful for your assessment, particularly regarding:

1. Limitations of harmonic superposition versus nonlinear dynamo models for forecast horizons exceeding one century.
2. Quantitative estimation of the stochastic triggering contribution to forecast uncertainty (in light of § 9.1 of your 2017 review).
