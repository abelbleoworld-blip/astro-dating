Dear Dr. Steinhilber,

Your 2013 paper with Dr. Beer ("Prediction of solar activity for the next 500 years," J. Geophys. Res.: Space Physics) has served as a key methodological reference for our work. We have extended the cosmogenic isotope approach by adding three observational proxies.

The SOL-1 pipeline combines:

1. IntCal20 Δ¹⁴C — 9,501 points, 55 kyr (as in your work);
2. GISP2 ¹⁰Be — 387 points, 40 kyr (as in your work);
3. Pre-telescopic sunspot records — 112 observations, Yau & Stephenson 1988 (new);
4. Auroral catalogues — ~2,000 records, Silverman 1992 / Keimatsu 1970 (new);
5. Eclipse corona morphology — 116 eclipses, Stephenson 1997 (new).

Cross-validation confirms the Gleissberg cycle (~88 yr) in four of four applicable observational proxies. The ¹⁰Be–Δ¹⁴C correlation is r = 0.47.

Our Monte Carlo forecast (500 realisations): median GSM at ~2126 CE (68 % CI: 2058–2206), consistent with your 2090 ± 30 CE prediction. The additional proxies narrow the 68 % confidence interval and provide independent confirmation.

We would welcome your feedback, particularly on:

1. Whether the addition of pre-telescopic direct observations meaningfully constrains the forecast.
2. How the harmonic superposition compares to your PCA-based approach.
3. Any concerns regarding the eclipse corona morphology proxy.
